About me

I find data science so fun, it really has become both a professional and personal passion of mine. I truly enjoy thinking deeply to define problems, digging through data in search of answers, creating structured models to test my theories, and highlighting my results in dashboards and visualizations. In my five years of work in economic consulting, I am pleased to have had countless opportunities to hone my data science skills and create real value for clients. Professionally, data science is my tool to learn, teach, and help.

Personally, there are so many things I am interested in, and I’ve always been this way. Growing up, my parents supported my imagination and fed my interests, and I have many pleasant memories of being taken to my favorite place: the public library. My favorite books were the Magic Tree House series, where the main characters were children with imaginations just like mine. They could be anybody and do anything. When I work with data, I feel the same way. When I clean data I am a detective. I thoroughly investigate, find patterns, identify things that don’t belong. When I run models I am an explorer. I search for answers, trust my experience, and have faith in math as my compass. When I present my results I am a Renaissance painter. I build elegant expressions of myself, toil to get every detail just right, and proudly showcase my work.
